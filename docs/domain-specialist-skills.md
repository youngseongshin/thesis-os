# Domain Specialist Skills

Thesis OS can use domain specialist skills when a generic research note is not enough.

Specialists do not replace Lattice judgment. They produce better evidence packets for Lattice.

## Semiconductor Specialist

The semiconductor specialist interprets AI infrastructure, memory, substrate, packaging, and equipment signals.

Typical questions:

- Is the signal about demand, supply, pricing, or inventory?
- Which part of the value chain is affected?
- Is this a direct beneficiary or second-order beneficiary?
- Is the proxy data strong enough to update a thesis?
- What would falsify the interpretation?

Example inputs:

- market snapshots
- export/import proxy data
- company filings and official releases
- analyst-report summaries
- supply-chain social clusters
- local DB features

Example outputs:

- specialist evidence packet
- value-chain map
- thesis update candidate
- uncertainty and attribution notes
- action queue questions

## Deep Alpha

Deep Alpha searches for variant perception.

It asks:

- What is the market underestimating?
- Which second-order beneficiary is not in the obvious basket?
- Is the signal measurable?
- What evidence gap must be closed before Lattice can act?
- What forward horizon should be evaluated?

Deep Alpha should produce candidates, not portfolio decisions.

## Deep Dive

Deep Dive turns a candidate into a structured research packet.

Minimum sections:

- one-line thesis
- why it surfaced
- evidence table
- assumptions
- base rates or analogs
- timing and setup
- valuation or expectation risk
- counterarguments
- invalidation
- Lattice handoff questions

## Devil's Advocate Pairing

Every high-importance specialist output should be paired with a devil's advocate review before action.

The pair should answer:

```text
Specialist: What could be true and underpriced?
Devil's Advocate: What must be wrong, priced in, too early, or unmeasurable?
Lattice: What action or prediction follows, if any?
```
